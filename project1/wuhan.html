Wuhan, China

My hometown Wuhan is a city in central China. It's a huge metropolis probably bigger than Chicago but is not very famous as Beijing or Shanghai.

Wuhan is divided into three parts by the Yangtze River and its largest tributary, Han river. Therefore, Wuhan is also called as "City of Rivers".

The city contains many skyscrapers, foreign concession buildings, and hundreds of lakes.

The Wuchang uprising in 1911 completely overthrows the five thousand years imperial rule of Chinese history. It is the proudest thing Wuhan history.

Bridges & Water

Since Wuhan is divided by rivers and lakes, there are many bridges in the city.

The first bridge across the Yangtze is built-in here in 1955, this bridge is the symbol of the city.

Visit

Unlike other Chinese cities, Wuhan is not famous for the fancy shopping mall and expensive restaurant.

Due to its location in central China, Wuhan combined the style of Northern and Southern China.

Welcome to Wuhan if you have a chance to visit China. Enjoy the delicious food and the atmosphere of common people.
